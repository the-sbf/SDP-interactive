Welcome
Why we're doing this
Read this before you dive in — it'll make the rest make sense.

Your last report came in under 60% in one or more of your subjects — and 60% is the bar SBF asks every scholar to clear in every subject. You're not here because we're disappointed. You're here because we want to help you get over that bar next term, and we think you can.

This plan is a chance to slow down and actually think about how the term went. Done well, a debrief like this helps you:

  • Reflect on how you really performed
  • Spot your strengths — and the bits that need work
  • Understand why your results came out the way they did
  • Set goals you can actually hit next term

Be straight with yourself — there are no wrong answers, and nobody's grading this. Tap Next when you're ready.

My Action Plan
What makes a goal SMART?
Five quick checks. If your goal passes all five, it's a real plan — not just a hope.
Specific — say exactly what you'll do
Measurable — a number you can check
Achievable — stretching, but possible
Relevant — it actually moves your mark
Time-bound — by when?
Vague ✗ "I want to do better at school."
Not specific. No number. No deadline. Nothing to check.
SMART ✓ "I will lift my overall average from 48% to 62% by the end of next term by studying 45 minutes a day, Monday to Friday, and going to extra Maths lessons every week."
Specific action. Measurable jump. Achievable. Relevant to the 60% target. Time-bound.

Your SMART goal
One sentence. It should pass all five SMART checks.

Quick SMART check
  • S — does it name a specific action?
  • M — is there a number (mark, hours, days)?
  • A — could you actually do this on a normal week?
  • R — does it move you toward 60%+?
  • T — is there a deadline?
